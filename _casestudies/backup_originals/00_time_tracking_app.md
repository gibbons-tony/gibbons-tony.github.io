## The Challenge

<div class="pull-quote float-right">
"An early-career project that, years later, became the foundation for billion-dollar P&L payroll allocation"
</div>

Early in my Google career, the organization lacked a systematic way to track what teams were working on. Annual planning required understanding resource allocation, but the process relied on manual surveys, spreadsheets, and tribal knowledge—creating inefficiency and inaccuracy.

Leaders needed to answer: "Where are teams spending their time?" and "How should we allocate resources for the next fiscal year?" Without structured data, these decisions were made with incomplete information.

## My Approach: Web App + Structured Process

I built an end-to-end solution combining technology and organizational change management:

**1. Built a Web Application**

Created a user-friendly web app that automated collection of what employees were working on:
- Structured data entry for projects, products, and time allocation
- Easy-to-use interface for non-technical employees
- Data validation and quality checks
- Integration with existing systems for reporting

**2. Designed Structured Process**

Technology alone wouldn't drive adoption—I needed an organizational process:
- Defined when and how employees should submit data
- Created training materials and documentation
- Established data governance and ownership models
- Built feedback loops for continuous improvement

**3. Scaled Company-Wide with PMO**

Partnered with the Program Management Office to scale beyond my immediate team:
- Aligned with annual planning cycles
- Secured executive sponsorship
- Rolled out across divisions systematically
- Integrated into official planning processes

**4. Executive Greenlight from Sundar Pichai**

The project's strategic value was recognized at the highest level—**Sundar Pichai himself greenlit the initiative**, providing the executive backing needed for company-wide adoption.

## The Outcome

The system became embedded in Google's annual planning process and continued to deliver value for years:

**Immediate Impact:**
- **Company-wide adoption**: Scaled across the organization as official resource tracking tool
- **Data-driven planning**: Annual planning based on actual time allocation data rather than estimates
- **Resource visibility**: Leaders could see where teams were investing effort in real-time
- **Planning efficiency**: Reduced time spent on manual data collection during planning cycles

**Long-Term Value (The Full-Circle Story):**

Years later, when I was building the [Cost & Profitability Framework](/casestudies/profitability_framework/), I discovered my time tracking system was **still in use**—its functionality had been integrated into the official 'moma' suite.

More remarkably, this early-career project became the **foundation for activity-based payroll allocation** in a multi-billion dollar P&L framework. The structured data on what teams were working on (which products, which customers) enabled unprecedented accuracy in allocating engineering costs—one of the largest expense categories in the business.

**The Lesson:**

This demonstrates the long-term value of well-designed systems that solve real problems with clean data models and scalable architecture. What started as a resource planning tool evolved into critical infrastructure for financial analytics—showing how foundational work compounds over time.

**Technical Achievement:**
- Full-stack web development (early in career)
- Organizational change management at scale
- Data model design that stood the test of time
- Integration thinking (built for extensibility, not just immediate use case)

The project showcased early initiative, systems thinking, and the ability to drive adoption of technical solutions through organizational change management—skills that would define my career trajectory at Google.
