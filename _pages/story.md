## The Foundation

My journey as an insatiably curious builder began in Oakland, where I learned drive and resilience watching my parents build businesses despite limited resources. This early exposure to entrepreneurship shaped my approach: build something from nothing, solve real problems, create lasting value.

I started at Intel designing chips—PCI Express controllers, low-power architectures, innovation at the silicon level. But I noticed that engineers executed what we were told to build. The real decisions—what to build, which markets to target, when to ship—came from strategy and finance teams. This insatiable curiosity led me to understand how those decisions were made—not to leave engineering, but to bridge technical and business worlds in ways most people can't.

## The Expansion

So I got my MBA in Finance. I learned how money shapes technology—how P&Ls drive product roadmaps, how market dynamics determine feature prioritization, how financial constraints shape technical architecture. At Google, I put that understanding to work.

At Google, I progressively moved through FP&A, TPM/Operations, and Data Analytics Architecture, building systems that connected the technical and financial worlds. The [revenue platform I built](/casestudies/03_revenue_platform/) grew from 0 to 8,000 daily active users, becoming the single source of truth for Google Cloud's $30B+ business. I created the [first credible customer P&L](/casestudies/05_profitability_framework/) with >100K SKUs, unlocking $200M+ in optimization opportunities. I [saved $100M+ in a single quarter](/casestudies/02_inventory_project/) combining root-cause analysis with machine learning to solve a data center inventory crisis.

The work earned recognition—including an R&D portfolio visibility system [greenlit by Sundar Pichai](/casestudies/00_time_tracking_app/) that addressed billions in spend with little control, gamifying tracking to provide unprecedented visibility—and years later became the foundation for payroll allocation in Google's multi-billion dollar P&L framework.

## How I Work: Creating Pull, Not Push

Through two decades of building platforms, I've learned that the most sustainable systems create pull rather than push. The Revenue Platform went from 0 to 8,000 users not because we forced adoption, but because we built something so essential that people couldn't work without it.

This philosophy shapes everything:
- **Build the landing pad before launching the rocket** — Ensure users are ready before deploying solutions
- **Create pull through excellence** — Make systems so good that adoption is organic
- **Bridge technical and business** — Speak both languages fluently
- **Ship first, perfect later** — Velocity with quality gates

I use what I call the "UAT as investment" strategy: have users validate that the solution works for THEIR specific use cases before launch. By the time we deploy, they've already done the leg work to integrate it into their workflows. They're not just users—they're investors who've proven it will work.

My cross-disciplinary background—silicon design, strategic finance, AI systems—gives me a unique lens. I see how money shapes technology decisions, how technical constraints impact business strategy, and how AI can bridge both worlds.

## The Next Frontier

With platforms serving 8,000+ users and delivering nine-figure impact, I saw where technology was heading. AI and ML weren't just incremental improvements—they would fundamentally change how systems could work. I wanted to be at the forefront of that shift.

I pursued UC Berkeley's Master's in Data Science to build deep technical expertise in the next wave. RAG systems, computer vision, NLP, cloud-native ML architectures—mastering the full stack from theory to production infrastructure. I applied these skills in real-time at Google Cloud, building AI-powered analytics assistants that let business users query billion-dollar datasets through natural language. The combination was powerful: two decades spanning silicon design, strategic finance, and platform architecture, now enhanced with cutting-edge AI capabilities.

My passion is building the system—especially when everyone says it can't be done."**
